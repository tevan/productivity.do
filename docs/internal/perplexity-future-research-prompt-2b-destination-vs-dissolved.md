### 2. The destination-app vs. dissolved-app thesis

Two competing futures:

- **A. Apps dissolve into agent surfaces.** ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini become the front door; productivity apps are commodity backends accessed via MCP. UI lives in the chat surface or in glanceable widgets (Apple Intelligence summaries, Pixel "At a Glance"). Apps that survive own *unique data* that can't be reconstituted from elsewhere.
- **B. Destination apps strengthen.** Generative UI is too noisy; users want a coherent surface they can muscle-memory through. Apps that win become the place specific decisions get made (Linear for engineering work, Cal.com for booking, Notion for docs).

What's the *evidence* for each? Are there sub-categories where one is clearly winning? Cite specific user-research or product-strategy posts where founders have publicly committed to one or the other.
