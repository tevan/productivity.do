Plan, capture, schedule. Nothing else.

Productivity is a day surface. The single place you go to plan a day, capture what's coming, and schedule the time it'll take. Three verbs. That's it.

Most "productivity stacks" sprawl into five apps because each one keeps growing into the others. Calendars try to become inboxes. Task managers try to become wikis. Inboxes try to become task managers. Everything reaches for everything else, and the user pays the cost in tab-juggling.

We took the opposite swing. Productivity is opinionated about what it isn't. No email inbox. No chat. No file storage. No wiki. The day surface is exactly the place those things aren't — so when you land here, you're planning, not reading email.

What we believe

  • One surface for the day, not five. Calendar, tasks, notes, and a Calendly-style booking page — collapsed into one keyboard-driven workspace.
  • Stay opinionated about scope. Email, chat, and file storage are great products that already exist. We integrate with them; we don't try to replace them.
  • Keyboard-first beats GUI-first for power users. Every action has a shortcut. Nobody has to click through three menus to reschedule a task.
  • Your data is yours. Self-host the backend, export everything as ICS or JSON, sync with Google so we're never the only copy.
  • Pricing should be honest. No "starting at" with feature unlocks four tiers up. Three plans, everything explained on the pricing page.
  • Security is table stakes. Atomic transactions, signed webhooks, scoped keys, timing-safe compares. The boring details done right.

Where we are

Productivity is in active development. We're building in public — the changelog tracks every shipped feature. The waitlist is open and we're onboarding solo professionals first.
