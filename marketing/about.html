Built by people who got tired of switching tabs.

Productivity started because the calendar/task/scheduling stack got out of hand.

You'd open Google Calendar to see your day. Open Todoist to remember what to do. Open Calendly to book a meeting. Open Reclaim to schedule deep work. Open Zapier to glue half of it together. Five tabs, four subscriptions, and three places where the same meeting could live in slightly different states.

We thought: what if the calendar was the workspace? Tasks live next to events. Booking pages share availability with focus blocks. AI scheduling considers your real workload. The API is real and signed and free. It's all one tool.

What we believe

  • Tools should integrate, not multiply. One subscription for the productivity stack, not five.
  • Keyboard-first beats GUI-first for power users. Every action has a shortcut. Nobody has to click through three menus to reschedule a task.
  • Your data is yours. Self-host the backend, export everything as ICS or JSON, sync with Google so we're never the only copy.
  • Pricing should be honest. No "starting at" with feature unlocks four tiers up. Three plans, everything explained on the pricing page.
  • Security is table stakes. Atomic transactions, signed webhooks, scoped keys, timing-safe compares. The boring details done right.

Where we are

Productivity is in active development. We're building in public — the changelog tracks every shipped feature. The waitlist is open and we're onboarding solo professionals first.
