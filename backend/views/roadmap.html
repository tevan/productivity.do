Strategic Roadmap · 2026

Be the place where someone — and the agents acting for them — decide what to do next.

productivity.do compresses your calendar, tasks, notes, and files into a single recommended next action — and exposes that same logic to ChatGPT, Claude, and Siri so the answer is wherever you are. Same answer in the app or via voice. We are the brain; the AI is the voice.

StagePre-launch · Charter validation BuiltSolo founder + AI-leverage Horizon~1 week to validation
01 · The thesis

The bet, in plain words

On the surface productivity.do looks like a calendar/tasks/notes app. That's intentional. The bet is what happens underneath — and how it shows up in AI assistants.

CLAIM 01

Storage and connectors are commoditizing

AWS Bedrock, Google Vertex, OpenAI + Snowflake all ship "talk to your data." Within 24 months, plugging GCal + Todoist directly into ChatGPT will be trivial.

CLAIM 02

Opinionated decision logic is not

The ranker that compresses calendar + tasks + project pins + intent + estimation history into one specific next action is hundreds of product judgments tuned against real users. AWS doesn't ship product judgment.

CLAIM 03

The surface IS how we capture the signals

A user won't pin projects in ChatGPT. They will in a calendar app. Looking like a productivity app is how we earn the right to capture the structured signals only we capture.

Same answer, wherever you are. We're the brain; ChatGPT is the voice.

03 · Where we're going

The 12-month surface

Open productivity.do — or ask ChatGPT, Claude, or Siri "what should I work on?" — and see one screen with one sentence.

Right now: prep for the 2pm sync with Anne. You have 50 minutes free. Sara sent the spec doc Tuesday — it's attached. Three open questions in your notes from last week's meeting are still unresolved. Start →

Click Start. Focus block lands on calendar from now until 1:55. Note opens, scrolled to unresolved questions. Spec doc is right there. When the meeting starts, the focus block ends naturally. After the meeting, the screen updates: "Your prep paid off — 2 decisions captured. Want to log them as tasks?"

Same answer reachable from ChatGPT. Same answer from Claude. From Siri. From any AI assistant that calls our MCP. Calendar / tasks / notes / booking pages are still here for users who prefer working in them directly — but the primary interaction is one screen, one decision, one click.

04 · The four investments

Four load-bearing investments

Each is concrete, demonstrable in under 60 seconds, and gets more valuable with use without requiring the user to adopt new behaviors. Cloning one of the four doesn't get the value — a competitor has to clone all four.

INV 01 · 1-2 days

Files unified across pillars

One file picker, one storage. Files attach to events, tasks, AND notes via the same mechanism. The same spec doc threads across the meeting, the prep task, the meeting notes — once. Per-file "appears in" rail.

INV 02 · 1-2 days

MCP workflow tools

Four agent-callable tools: plan_today, triage_inbox, summarize_project, rebalance_week. Workflow-named, not table-named. CRUD parity loses agent routing to workflow uniqueness.

INV 03 · 2-3 days

"What should I do right now" surface

Press Space anywhere → one screen, one sentence, one Start button. Deterministic ranker. Auditable: every recommendation explainable in one sentence with three parts (why this, why now, what would change the answer).

INV 04 · 1-2 days

Cross-pillar timeline

Every event/task/note/file/comment/edit becomes a row on a unified per-day or per-project timeline. Notion can't (page-centric). Todoist can't (task-only). Apple Notes can't.

The signals only we capture

A user connecting GCal + Todoist directly to ChatGPT gives ChatGPT events and tasks. A user using productivity.do for 60 days gives our ranker an entirely different layer.

What we don't capture (deliberately)

Always-on listening, screen recording / OCR, email ingestion, app-tracking, dwell-time analytics, location. The "AI watches everything" approach is contested (Microsoft Recall, Limitless, Rewind all face friction). The "user gives us structured signals via the workflow they already do" approach is uncontested.

07 · A familiar shape, inverted

Same shape as a social media algorithm — opposite incentives

The mechanism rhymes: weighted scoring over captured signals, ranked output, per-user personalization. Five differences invert the optimization target.

Inverted incentive

TikTok maximizes time-in-app. We minimize time-to-close. Success = "user opens, clicks Start within 5s, leaves." That's a TikTok failure.

Explicit vs. implicit signals

TikTok captures dwell + scroll + micro-engagement. We capture pins + intent + priority. Implicit drifts to lowest common denominator. Explicit reflects deliberate intent.

Stated vs. revealed preference

TikTok bets revealed beats stated. True in entertainment; poisonous in productivity. A user says they want to ship; they actually scroll. We honor the stated.

Open vs. closed deployment

TikTok's algorithm only runs inside TikTok. Ours runs inside our app AND exposes via MCP to ChatGPT, Claude, Siri. We don't trap; we travel.

The cleaner pitch: Same shape, opposite incentives. Social media uses your behavior to maximize time spent. We use your stated preferences to minimize it. Opaque and addictive vs. explainable and built to get you out the door.
08 · The build path

Build sequence to charter validation

~1 week of focused work, plus launch-checklist completion. Realistic timelines for a single founder with AI-leverage — days, not weeks.

Pre-build · launch-checklist plumbing

0-2 days

Stripe Price IDs, Resend domain, Sentry DSN, Anthropic key, Cloudflare SSL Full(strict). Charter users hit failures if any of these 503.

Files unified across pillars + MCP workflow tools

DAY 1-2 · parallel

One picker, one storage, threading across events/tasks/notes. Same week: plan_today, triage_inbox, summarize_project wrapping existing pure functions. Documented at /help/api/mcp-workflows.

"What should I do right now" surface

DAY 3

TodayPanel becomes the primary surface. Three-part explanation per recommendation. One-click Start: focus block + linked resource + attached files. Space hotkey from anywhere. Phone breakpoint becomes default landing.

Trust-state instrumentation + cross-pillar timeline

DAY 4

recommendation_events table capturing accepted/dismissed/manually_overridden/started_but_abandoned/re_ranked. Founder dashboard at /admin/recommendations. Plus the per-day and per-project timeline.

Polish + onboarding scripts

DAY 5

Welcome email sequence (signup → day 3 → day 7). 5-slide onboarding deck. Daily review cadence: 9am dashboard check.

Charter validation window

~30 DAYS · time-bound

Ship to 6-10 specific people. Founder-as-PM mode: phone number, daily/weekly check-ins, fast iteration. Monday: review dashboard, ship fixes by EOD. Wednesday: 30-min call with 1-2 charter users. Friday: weekly summary email.

09 · Getting the first users

Charter recruitment plan

The hardest unsolved problem. Target: 6-10 specific people in 60 days. Personalized outreach, no template, no public launch.

The profile (4 of 5)

  • Currently uses Google Calendar and a task system (Todoist, Asana, Linear, Things)
  • Already uses ChatGPT or Claude for work (Pro tier preferred)
  • Self-describes as "overwhelmed" or "always behind on planning"
  • Has 5+ meetings per week and 20+ active tasks
  • Knows the founder personally or 1 degree separated (so feedback is candid)

NOT good charter user fits

  • Already uses a system they love (Things + Apple Notes; Notion-as-OS)
  • Single-tool simplicity preference
  • Doesn't use AI assistants for work

The funnel · 60 days

6-10

Week 1-2 — Build the list

30 candidates from professional network, personal network, public productivity-tool complainers (max 3-5), existing single-user product alumni.

Week 3-4 — Outreach

Personalized message via the channel they actually use (text, email, DM — not LinkedIn). 3 sentences: what we built, why I thought of them specifically, ask for 30 min. No demo links upfront.

Week 5-6 — Onboarding

30-min call. Walk through the surface live. Get them to pin 2 projects + write one intent line on the call. 14-day Pro comp. Founder's phone number. Private group with other charter users.

Week 7+ — Engagement

Daily dashboard review. Wednesday call with 1-2 different charter users each week. Friday email: "What did the product get right? What did it get wrong?"

What we explicitly DON'T do

  • Public launch announcement (Product Hunt, X) — burns the launch story before ready
  • Cold outreach to email lists — spam shape
  • "Beta access waitlist" — creates pressure to ship before ready
  • Content marketing as recruitment — slow burn, won't yield 6 users in 60 days
  • Discord / community building — too early; <100 users would feel empty
10 · The 30-day learning loop

What "validation" actually means

We're not testing "is the ranker perfect?" We're testing "can users understand and correct it fast enough to trust it tomorrow?"

For each recommendation surfaced to a charter user, capture five trust-state signals. Each is a different read on user trust.

EventMeaningHealth
AcceptedClicked Start, started doing the thingTrust building
DismissedClicked the x — wrong for this momentHealthy disagreement
Re-rankedAsked for a different recommendationHealthy disagreement
Started but abandonedClicked Start, didn't finishFragile trust
Manually overriddenDid something else from calendar/tasksSilent disengagement

Manually overridden is the worst signal — silent disengagement, user has stopped trusting us enough to even disagree. Accepted with completion is the gold-standard signal.

Weekly check-in questions

  • "What did the product get right this week?"
  • "What did it get wrong, and how did you correct it?"
  • "If you had to describe what this product does to a colleague, what would you say?" (positioning test)
11 · 90-day check

What "winning" looks like

Three signals to watch in the 90 days post-launch. If they land, the strategy is working. If they don't, which one DID land tells us what to invest in.

SIGNAL 01

Daily habit

Charter users say "I check this every morning." Behavior is the moat, not the data.

SIGNAL 02

Agent surface works

A charter user, unprompted: "I just told ChatGPT to plan my day and it called you."

SIGNAL 03

Word-of-mouth

Someone outside the charter group signs up because their colleague uses it.

If signals don't land — diagnostic

  • Only #1 lands: behavior moat works, agent surface needs more investment.
  • Only #2 lands: people use us through ChatGPT, not directly — destination thesis is wrong.
  • Only #3 lands: social discovery works but daily-decision moment isn't sticky — different product.
  • None land: thesis is wrong. Re-evaluate.
12 · What could go wrong

Risks, ranked

Honest list. The one most likely to kill the company isn't the technical one.

Kills the co.

Users don't actually want time-to-close. The deepest risk. The whole strategy assumes users prefer "see the answer, click Start, leave." But the engagement loop is addictive for a reason. If charter users say "this is cool" and don't check it daily, the bet collapses at the level of user need.

Kills the co.

No charter users care enough to be charter users. Strategy depends on 6-10 specific people in the network. If we can't recruit them, we have a distribution problem before we have a product problem.

Wounds

Anthropic / OpenAI ship "plan my day" first-party. Could land in 2 months, not 5 years. Mitigation: the race is structured-data accumulation, not feature parity. We become a destination they call, not a competitor.

Wounds

MCP standard fragments. Today MCP is winning, but young. If Anthropic, OpenAI, Google diverge into proprietary protocols, we'd ship 3-4 versions of the same workflow tools. Annoying, not fatal.

Survive

AI commoditization erodes single-purpose AI features. Otter saw this. Mem saw this. Hedge: don't ship features whose moat is "we use AI to do X." Ship features whose moat is "your stuff is here and connected."

Survive

Big-platform consolidation. Apple Intelligence routing to Reminders by default. Mitigation: prioritize MCP (cross-platform) over App Intents (Apple-only) for now.

Self-inflicted

Scope creep into Notion-shape. Custom databases, sub-projects, formulas. Strategy doc encodes the answer: no, even when asked.

Self-inflicted

Adding engagement mechanics. Streaks, badges, "your week in review." TikTok-shape drift. Reject on sight.

13 · The falsifiability check

What would change my mind

Strong strategy is falsifiable. Each load-bearing claim has evidence that would force a pivot.

Abandon "time-to-close" as the metric IF

Charter users explicitly prefer time-in-app · D-active rate <40% despite product working as designed · Multiple users ask for streaks/badges/reactivation emails. Two of three → wrong metric.

Abandon "destination app" framing IF

Most users access only through ChatGPT MCP calls; the app sees <2 sessions/week. Next-thing surface used once and dismissed. If true → strip UI, lean into agent backend.

Abandon "surface IS data-capture mechanism" IF

Users don't pin projects, don't write intent lines, don't set estimates. The ranker without those signals produces answers no better than ChatGPT given raw GCal/Todoist. If true → substrate moat doesn't exist.

Abandon the productivity vertical IF

After 6 months: retention <industry-benchmark D30 for productivity SaaS · No one independently names same weekly pain · Word-of-mouth doesn't fire. If true → vertical is wrong, pattern might still be right.

Important distinction: "Users don't want time-to-close" (strategy is wrong) and "users want time-to-close but don't trust this implementation" (execution is the problem) are different problems. Before abandoning the metric, test execution: are recommendations explainable enough? Is Start friction low enough? Trust comes from repeated "yes, that's right" moments — if those aren't happening, fix execution before pivoting strategy.
14 · What we deliberately don't build

What we won't chase

Each item is a temptation we resist. The list is at least as important as the build list.

Notion-shape

Custom databases, properties, formulas. Top-level Projects pillar with dashboards. Wiki/docs hierarchy. Insight dashboards as primary surfaces.

Engagement-maxxing

Streak counters. "Your week in review" scrolls. Completion badges. Reactivation emails. Push notifications about peer activity.

Voice-overreach

Always-on listening. Wake words. Multi-step orchestration via voice. Voice without preview-and-confirm.

Aggregator shape

"Universal productivity agent that connects all your apps." Three companies tried (Rube/Composio, Zapier Central, IFTTT AI). All retreated.

Surveillance

Screen recording / OCR / Recall-style. Email ingestion beyond opt-in forwarding. App-tracking. Location. Dwell-time analytics.

Premature abstraction

CRDT-based sync. Multi-vertical platform play before any single vertical works. User-authored DSLs, formulas, JS sandboxes.
